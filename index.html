After moving around frequently for years my Family settled in Northern Virginia, where I spent most of my youth. Even before pursuing a degree, I was deeply entrenched in the world of computer science and STEM. I am a four time VEX-robotics world tournament competitor and state champion in Virginia. These years on the robotics team were my introduction to the world of programming, via RobotC, a proprietary derivative of the C programming language. This is also when I began my German studies and I quickly became proficient, completing a three week total German language immersion camp after just three years of study. Before I had even applied to schools, I knew that I would pursue these two passions further. Now, nearing the end of my four year education, I am capable of fluent German interactions in casual and academic settings, as well as being proficient in a number of different programming languages with a wide array of projects of experience. These projects range from simple python data structures, to an application first created in Java for desktop use that was later ported to android, and a custom created C 3D ray tracing engine capable of multiple sources of light and shadow.
